BOOSTING MULTIAGENT REINFORCEMENT LEARNING VIA PERMUTATION INVARIANT AND PERMUTATION EQUIVARIANT NETWORKS
Unsupervised reinforcement learning (URL) poses a promising paradigm to learn useful behaviors in a task-agnostic environment without the guidance of extrinsic rewards to facilitate the fast adaptation of various downstream tasks. Previous works focused on the pre-training in a model-free manner while lacking the study of transition dynamics modeling that leaves a large space for the improvement of sample efficiency in downstream tasks.
